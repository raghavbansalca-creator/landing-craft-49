Direction & Significance

Each concept below embeds a directional or conceptual meaning into the "7" mark — so the logo says something, not just looks good.

Direction 01
Arrowhead Terminus
Forward Purposeful
The Concept The dot at the top-left is the origin — where you are now. The "7" stroke is the path. The arrowhead at the end is the destination. The whole mark reads as a journey with intention: a firm that knows where it's taking you. "Vision" in the name earns its place — you can see the direction.
Direction 02
Rising J-Curve
The stroke descends then hooks upward at the base — the hockey stick / J-curve shape that every CFO knows. Means: things turn around, performance recovers. The firm that helps you get there.
GrowthFP&A
Direction 03
Tapered Brush Stroke
Stroke starts thin (origin) and grows thick (destination) — like a calligrapher's brush moving with momentum. Weight = direction. The mark feels alive and in motion.
MomentumCalligraphic
Direction 04
Motion Echo
Two faded ghost strokes trail behind the main "7" — a motion blur effect. The mark is clearly in forward motion, not standing still. Speed and trajectory.
VelocityDynamic
Direction 05
North Star Origin
The origin dot becomes a North Star — a guiding light. The "7" path flows from it. Directly ties into "Vision": a firm with a clear north star, helping clients navigate toward theirs.

Direction 06
Growing Dot Trail
The "7" is made entirely of dots that grow from small → large. Direction is unmistakable. Each dot = a milestone. The path from origin to destination is visible at a glance.
